Introduction

The craft of a well-built webpage

HTML is the quiet skeleton beneath every page on the web — invisible when done right, unmistakable when done wrong. It gives structure to ideas, hierarchy to information, and meaning to the words that fill a screen.

Good design is as little design as possible — but never less than what the content demands.

A heading announces intent. A paragraph develops it. Together, they form the smallest complete unit of written thought on the web — simple in form, limitless in expression.
